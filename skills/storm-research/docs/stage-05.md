## Stage 05: Simulated Peer Review

### Purpose

This stage critiques the draft outline to find weak arguments and logical gaps. It acts as a quality control mechanism before the final text is written. This simulated review process strengthens the final report by addressing potential criticisms early.

### Inputs

* The detailed outline from Stage 04.
* The contradiction and tension map from Stage 03.
* The original scope document from Stage 01.
* The gathered research data and sources.

### Actions

1. Adopt a critical, devil's advocate persona to review the outline.
2. Scrutinize the logical flow for gaps or unsupported leaps in reasoning.
3. Identify claims in the outline that lack sufficient evidence or citation mapping.
4. Check if the outline handles the tensions mapped in Stage 03.
5. Look for biases or over-reliance on a single expert perspective.
6. Suggest specific improvements, additions, or structural changes to strengthen the narrative.
7. Flag any sections that drift outside the original scope boundaries.
8. Compile the critiques and suggestions into a formal review document.
9. Revise the outline based on the peer review feedback.

### Outputs

* A formal peer review document detailing critiques and suggestions.
* A list of identified weak arguments or logical gaps.
* Specific recommendations for structural or content improvements.
* A revised outline that incorporates the review feedback.

### QA Checklist

* [ ] Did the review identify at least one area for improvement in the outline?
* [ ] Are the critiques specific and actionable?
* [ ] Does the review check for adequate coverage of mapped tensions?
* [ ] Were unsupported claims flagged for additional evidence?
* [ ] Has the outline been successfully revised based on the feedback?
* [ ] Does the revised outline still align with the original scope?
