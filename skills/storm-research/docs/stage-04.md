## Stage 04: Information Synthesis & Outline Drafting

### Purpose

This stage builds a structured narrative from the gathered findings. It organizes the raw data, persona insights, and tension maps into a logical flow. This ensures the final report is coherent, easy to read, and directly answers the original thesis question.

### Inputs

* The formal scope document from Stage 01.
* The persona questions and answers from Stage 02.
* The contradiction and tension map from Stage 03.
* All gathered research data and source links.

### Actions

1. Review all inputs to identify the most critical themes and findings.
2. Group related information into logical categories.
3. Develop a hierarchical outline with clear headings and subheadings.
4. Ensure the outline directly addresses the thesis question and sub-questions.
5. Integrate the different expert perspectives naturally into the structure.
6. Place the mapped tensions and contradictions in appropriate sections to provide balanced coverage.
7. Draft the core narrative flow, ensuring smooth transitions between sections.
8. Map specific sources and citations to the relevant sections of the outline.
9. Save the detailed outline to the workspace for the next stage.

### Outputs

* A comprehensive, hierarchical outline for the final report.
* A clear narrative structure with defined sections.
* A mapping of key findings and tensions to specific outline sections.
* A preliminary list of citations organized by section.

### QA Checklist

* [ ] Does the outline directly answer the main thesis question?
* [ ] Are all major themes from the research represented in the headings?
* [ ] Is the logical flow clear and easy to follow?
* [ ] Are the mapped tensions included in the structure?
* [ ] Are sources mapped to the sections where they will be cited?
* [ ] Does the outline stay within the boundaries defined in Stage 01?
