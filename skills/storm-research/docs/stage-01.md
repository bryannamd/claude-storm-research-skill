## Stage 01: Scope Clarification

### Purpose

This stage transforms a broad user request into a precise research question. It defines the exact boundaries of the investigation. This prevents scope creep and ensures the final report actually answers what you need to know. A well-defined scope is the foundation of the entire STORM process. Without it, subsequent stages will waste time on irrelevant tangents.

### Inputs

* The initial user prompt or research request.
* Any constraints provided by the user, such as timeframes or specific focus areas.
* Existing context from the current workspace or conversation history.
* Target audience information if specified.
* Desired output format preferences.

### Actions

1. Analyze the initial request to identify vague terms or overly broad concepts.
2. Break down the core topic into three to five specific, answerable sub-questions.
3. Define explicit boundaries for what the research will cover.
4. Define explicit boundaries for what the research will exclude.
5. Formulate a single, clear thesis question that guides the rest of the pipeline.
6. Identify key terminology that needs strict definitions.
7. Present the clarified scope to the user and obtain approval before proceeding to Stage 02.
8. Document the agreed scope in the workspace for downstream stages.

### Outputs

* A defined thesis question.
* A list of specific sub-questions to investigate.
* Explicit inclusion and exclusion criteria.
* A brief summary of the research goals.
* A glossary of key terms for the research context.
* A formal scope document saved to the workspace.

### QA Checklist

* [ ] Does the thesis question have a clear, objective answer?
* [ ] Are the exclusion criteria specific enough to prevent scope creep?
* [ ] Can the sub-questions be answered using available public information?
* [ ] Is the scope narrow enough to cover deeply in a standard report?
* [ ] Are all vague terms from the original prompt clearly defined?
* [ ] Has the user approved the scope and boundaries before Stage 02 begins?
* [ ] Has the scope document been saved successfully?
